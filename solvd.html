The loop

Four steps from a stuck problem to a proven fix.

01

Post

Frame a real, unsolved problem with its context and constraints. Attach an optional bounty to draw the right builders.

02

Build

Contributors propose and prototype solutions in a shared workspace, with code, designs, or written approaches side by side.

03

Discuss

Each solution carries its own thread. The community votes, stress-tests, and refines until the strongest approaches surface.

04

Adopt

The poster marks what shipped. Verified outcomes turn opinion into data and release the bounty to whoever earned it.

What sets it apart

Not a forum. A system for getting things solved.

01

Funded bounties

Posters escrow a reward that the platform releases to the adopted solution. Real money aligns real effort.

02

Verified outcomes

Every adoption is recorded with its result, so the winning solution is proven rather than merely upvoted.

03

Solver reputation

A portable, public record of solutions that worked, usable as a hiring and credibility signal.

04

AI synthesis

Near-duplicate solutions are clustered and summarized automatically, surfacing genuine consensus from noise.

05

Build workspace

Prototype the real thing by attaching code, docs, or designs instead of describing an idea in prose.

06

Private team spaces

Run the same loop internally. Teams post their own problems to a vetted, NDA-bound talent pool.

Defensibility

The moat is the data, not the chatter.

01Owns the outcome graph

Every solved problem adds a record of which solutions were tried, which won, and why. Over time this becomes a knowledge base no competitor holds.

02Solves the cold start

Bounty-funded problems attract solvers from day one. We launch narrow, in one vertical, and seed it manually rather than opening to problems in general.

03Compounding network effects

More solved problems attract more solvers, and more solvers attract more posters. Each loop makes the next one easier.

04Quality stays high

Reputation-weighted voting, automated deduplication, and verified-outcome badges keep spam and duplicate noise out of the way.
